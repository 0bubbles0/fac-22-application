Background

During the studies for my BSc Economics at SOAS, University of London (School of Oriental and African Studies), I learned to:

  • Research new topics in collaboration as well as independently.
  • Solve real-life problems analytically through abstract models.
  • Understand the causes and societal cost of unequal opportunities and unused potential, as well as international development issues, incentives and interactions between individuals, businesses and societies.
  • Enjoy the international environment and diversity of ideas from teachers and peers, around 50% of us being international, from all walks of life, cultures, ages, classes and personal identities.
  • Value the creativity and brain power that can be sparked through respectful, open-minded dialogue.

Discovering Coding through a random advert for the Google Grasshopper app felt:

  • Immediately comfortable and logical.
  • Like lightning struck – why had no one shown me this at school?
  • Inviting – I had always assumed it a secret club for teenage boys.
  • Exciting to imagine the life-hacks I might learn to create with a few lines of code!
  • Like a path to get my life unstuck from a few lost years.

My Personal Values

Through improving my coding skills, I hope to:

  • Contribute in preparing us for a better future.
  • Play an active part in fully digitising society, opening it up to less tech-savvy consumers.
  • Support people and organisations already on that path, lend my brain-power to “good tech”.

I wish to contribute to technology that enables people to thrive and is:

  • Accessible & Inclusive: affordable/free, proactively non-biased, open to all physical and learning abilities.
  • Sustainable: anticipating impacts on the environment, society, mental and physical health.
  • Responsible: consensual, considerate of data and consumer protection, protecting vulnerable groups, preventing misinformation.
  • Supportive: strengthening community spirit and personal wellness (financial, physical, emotional).
  • Bridging opportunity gaps.
  • Solving everyday problems for ordinary people.
  • Easing decision fatigue and the burden of emotional labour, especially for women and underfunded communities.

Founders and Coders

A place in the Founders and Coders Cohort 22 in Autumn 2021 would be an incredible opportunity to continue pursuing these goals in tandem with others.

  • Participating in the Coaching Programme and especially working with the Dragonfly Study Group has already felt like a Bootcamp. The last few weeks supercharged my progress and motivation, gave me more support and confidence on the path to being a developer than I could have imagined.
  • In the group, we have already had several fantastic “aha!” moments and it would be an absolute privilege to continue learning together, to keep up the pace, have some progress accountability and explore collaborating on projects and GitHub. While creating this website, I already considered several times how tasks might have been shared with partners. It would be exciting and immensely valuable to be able to mix my analytical problem-solving aptitude with the fantastic visual design creativity of my fellow current coachees.

To continue as part of the community

  • It felt safe to be invited to an environment that values mutual supportiveness over competitiveness. The special emphasis on pastoral care and respectful and encouraging communication has made a lasting impact on how I wish to conduct teamwork, which values and inter-personal dynamics I will be more proactive in fostering in work environments going forward. Meeting someone with different life experiences is an opportunity to learn, not a complication!
  • Already, I have greatly benefited from countless alumni and feel inspired to continue carrying the torch. On top of being eager to pass on everything I have learned so far to anyone who will listen, I would be especially excited to be involved in workshops for complete beginners – writing first HTML in a text editor or starting the first Codepen. Because I am convinced that coding could be a path for others to get themselves out of much stickier life situations than mine.

The Structure of the Course and Apprenticeship would provide the invaluable

  • guidance on technical employer interviews during the Pre-apprenticeship that could help overcome my CV and confidence issues.
  • financial security and peace of mind for me to fully absorb into the Bootcamp.
  • full package of Bootcamp, certification and 1-2 years of experience to set me on the career path I would enjoy.
